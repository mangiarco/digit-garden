Before diving deep into what a **second brain** is and what it's used for—and more specifically into what I think it is and how it can serve us—let me take a moment to share the story behind my decision to build it, as well as the purpose of this space overall.

# Good News and Problem Recognition

Probably you don’t know this, or maybe you’re not even interested, but I usually used Google Chrome as a browser on all my devices. I always had a lot of tabs open on both my smartphone (I’m always at the +99 counter) and PC. When a colleague of mine showed me (_thank you Szymon_ 🖤) the [Google Chrome feature that allows you to group tabs](https://blog.google/products/chrome/manage-tabs-with-google-chrome/), my first honest thought was: _**This is gonna change my life!**_

<img src="https://storage.googleapis.com/gweb-uniblog-publish-prod/original_images/Chrome_Tab-Groups_In-Line-Image-v7.gif"/>

And it was true! I discovered a super useful feature that I still use while browsing—but what I was referring to at that time was something different.

Back then, I thought what I had found was a solution to the information management issues that had afflicted me for a long time. What a fool 🤦‍♂️. Over the years, I tried many solutions to track information from different sources, not only digital workarounds—using some mobile apps (like **Evernote**) or Google Workspace solutions (like **Keep** + **Tasks**)—but also analog ones (like pen & paper, with **Ryder Carroll’s Bullet Journal method**). For a while, I lived symbiotically with **Notion**, for sure the most wonderful productivity and note-taking web application ever created, but nothing changed. For a short period, I even tried using the main engine of modern technology, **social media** (sharing everything privately on Facebook, or sending stuff to myself on WhatsApp—I still do this).

<img src="https://media0.giphy.com/media/Ty9Sg8oHghPWg/giphy.gif?cid=7941fdc63t1jxy6wimiaphfetbkvd1c2e19etn8g03cptwik&ep=v1_gifs_search&rid=giphy.gif&ct=g"/>

**Nothing worked for me.** And that was sad because I’m super curious about the world around me, and I cannot imagine how much information, how many images, thoughts, documents, videos, podcasts, and posts I’ve lost <b>forever</b> during all these failing years!!

But the best stories have happy endings, and this one does too. Because in trying to solve my problem, nothing worked—except **Obsidian**.  
I have to admit: I discovered Obsidian too late (_thank you Lele_ 🖤). It’s hands down the most versatile, powerful, free, and simple-to-use note-taking application the web has ever seen. But there’s no place for regret here! My mistakes can be opportunities for everyone who’s reading these words, and also for myself, because as a Chinese proverb says:

> «The best time to plant a tree was 20 years ago. The second best time is now.»

# What is a “Second Brain”?

![[my_second_brain_at_2025-09_26.png]]

First of all, credits: the idea of a Second Brain comes from **Tiago Forte**, who simply describes it as «_a useful system to manage personal information, with the aim of helping in gathering, organizing, and recalling ideas and knowledge in the most efficient way possible_».

This system literally works as an extension of our real brain and memory, allowing us to free our minds from mnemonic work and focus on creative and strategic thinking. In our times, as we know, managing information efficiently is crucial to ensure productivity and personal growth. Living without such a tool during the information era is a (losing) choice from multiple perspectives—not only productivity, but also memory and knowledge _tout court_.

I know this is not the best note to give you the idea on what a second brain really is, but it's mine point of view, and of course if you remember well you're in my [[digital garden]], so my granden my rules :)
